Early detection of dampness in walls and ceillings is critical for building maintenance and the prevention of structural damage. 
Dampness can occur through various mechanisms, such as filtration, condensation, or capillary action, 
and each type has different implications for the durability of materials and the preservation of the building.

The objective of this project is to develop a machine learning-based computer vision model capable of identifying and
classifying different types of dampness in wall images. 
This would allow for the automation of visual inspections, reduce human error, and facilitate more accurate architectural diagnoses.
